The Indian Ghats

The Indian Ghats are a series of mountain ranges and slopes found along the eastern and western coasts of India. They are divided into the Western Ghats and the Eastern Ghats.

Western Ghats

The Western Ghats, also known as the Sahyadri Hills, run parallel to the western coast of India. Key features include:

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site due to rich biodiversity.
  • Home to dense forests, numerous rivers, and waterfalls.
  • Popular hill stations like Munnar, Ooty, and Mahabaleshwar.

Eastern Ghats

The Eastern Ghats are a discontinuous range of mountains along the eastern coast. Key features include:

  • Lower in elevation compared to the Western Ghats.
  • Known for ancient temples like those in Tirupati and Bhubaneswar.
  • Important rivers like the Godavari and Krishna flow through these ranges.

The Ghats are crucial for India's biodiversity, water resources, and cultural heritage, making them an integral part of the nation's geography.
